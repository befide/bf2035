---
title: On the importance of controlled vocabularies
abstract: Four of the 343 research priorities listed on the Research Map of the Hochschulrektorenkonferenz are related to acelerator science. While the database is searchable by key terms, each of the four accelerator science-related priorities uses a different one.
---

With the bilingual research map, the University Rectors' Conference (HRK) presents the thematic research priorities of the German universities. It makes it possible to search for research-intensive topics that shape the profile of a university. The topics can be filtered professionally and regionally.

The research map of the University Rectors' Conference presents outstanding research priorities of the German universities in a clear and uniform manner. The universities thus provide information together, but on their own responsibility, about their research-intensive topics.

Restrictive conditions for admission are

- Each institutionally profiling research priorities of a university is supported by at least 25 cooperating professorships. -
  Up to eight research focusses per university are included in the database.

At the end of February, the database included 343 entries, of which only four related to the field of accelerator research.

## Critical comments

### Lack of coherence

The explicit aim of the database is to be accessible to non-experts. As such, the German version of the website promises a presentation "in a uniform manner". Less ambitiously, the English version only aims for "a standardised format". This appears to be a more fitting description, as little effort seems to have been made to use a controlled vocabulary for the key terms.

The result is a maximum of incoherence, as for each of the four research priorities a different key term is used to link it to accelerator research:

- Theoretical accelerator physics (theoretische Beschleunigerphysik)
- Accelerator development (Beschleunigerentwicklung)
- Accelerator Technology (Beschleunigertechnik)
- Laser-Plasma Acceleration (Laser-Plasma-Beschleunigung)
